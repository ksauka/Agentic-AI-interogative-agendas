# Strategic Hiring Role Description

## Strategic Talent Operations Partner

### Section 1. Short Title and Role Designation
**1.1 Short Title.** This document shall be cited as the **Role Description for Strategic Talent Operations Partner**.
**1.2 Role Designation.** The role governed by this document is the position of **Strategic Talent Operations Partner**.
**1.3 Nature of Role.** This is a strategically important coordination and execution role within a growing SME, with responsibilities spanning talent operations, structured hiring support, stakeholder coordination, and process reliability.

### Section 2. Organisational Context
**2.1 Company Context.** The hiring organisation is a growth-oriented SME preparing for expansion into additional markets within the next twelve months.
**2.2 Operating Conditions.** The organisation operates with a lean structure, limited managerial slack, and a need for reliable internal coordination across multiple functions.
**2.3 Strategic Relevance of the Role.** The role supports scalable talent-related processes, internal execution, and consistent operational workflows during growth.
**2.4 Human Accountability Context.** Selection requires careful review and shall not be reduced to simple keyword matching or automated recommendation alone.

### Section 3. Role Purpose
**3.1 Primary Purpose.** The role supports organisational growth by coordinating talent-related and operational processes in a structured, reliable, and scalable manner.
**3.2 Functional Positioning.** It sits between leadership, hiring execution, internal coordination, and process continuity, supporting decisions where multiple priorities must be managed simultaneously.
**3.3 Strategic Function.** It strengthens internal talent operations and coordination systems that support sustainable organisational expansion.

### Section 4. Core Responsibilities
**4.1 Coordination Responsibilities.** Coordinate hiring-related and talent-operations workflows across internal stakeholders.
**4.2 Process Responsibilities.** Support structured screening, tracking, scheduling, and follow-up processes while maintaining clarity and consistency.
**4.3 Communication Responsibilities.** Manage communication across leadership, hiring participants, candidates, and operational stakeholders reliably.
**4.4 Documentation Responsibilities.** Maintain records, process notes, workflow documentation, and follow-up structures necessary for effective execution.
**4.5 Improvement Responsibilities.** Identify workflow bottlenecks, coordination failures, or procedural inconsistencies and recommend practical improvements.
**4.6 Autonomy Requirement.** Operate with limited supervision while maintaining procedural discipline and stakeholder responsiveness.

### Section 5. Required Qualifications
**5.1 General Rule.** The following qualifications are required for initial screening purposes.
**5.2 Required Qualification 1: Process Coordination.** Demonstrate experience coordinating multi-step operational, talent-related, or workflow-driven processes.
**5.3 Required Qualification 2: Communication Capacity.** Demonstrate strong written and verbal communication appropriate for cross-functional coordination and structured follow-up.
**5.4 Required Qualification 3: Structured Evaluation Support.** Demonstrate evidence of structured evaluation, tracking, screening, scheduling, or comparable coordination work involving multi-stage judgement or review processes.
**5.5 Required Qualification 4: Stakeholder Management.** Demonstrate ability to work across multiple internal or external stakeholders and maintain continuity across moving priorities.
**5.6 Required Qualification 5: Independent Execution.** Demonstrate capacity to operate with limited supervision and sustain reliable execution over time.
**5.7 Required Qualification 6: SME or Fast-Paced Environment Experience.** Demonstrate experience within an SME, startup, or comparably fast-moving organisational environment where flexibility and follow-through are necessary.

### Section 6. Preferred Qualifications
**6.1 General Rule.** These qualifications are preferred rather than strictly required; their absence alone shall not justify rejection.
**6.2 Direct Talent Operations or Recruitment Coordination Experience.** Preference is given for direct recruitment operations, talent coordination, structured candidate handling, or hiring workflow support.
**6.3 Workflow Improvement Experience.** Preference is given for evidence of improving internal workflows, process structures, or organisational coordination routines.
**6.4 Assessment Criteria Experience.** Preference is given for work with assessment criteria, structured evaluation notes, screening records, or decision-support templates.
**6.5 Tool Exposure.** Preference is given for exposure to applicant tracking systems, workflow management tools, structured scheduling tools, or related digital systems.
**6.6 Fairness-Sensitive Judgement.** Preference is given for balancing operational efficiency with procedural care in talent-related or screening tasks.

### Section 7. Transferable Evidence Clause
**7.1 Recognition of Non-Identical Wording.** A candidate shall not be treated as unqualified merely because the CV does not repeat the exact language used in this role description.
**7.2 Transferable Competence Rule.** Where prior work demonstrates equivalent competence through adjacent experience, such evidence may satisfy a required or preferred qualification for screening purposes.
**7.3 Examples of Transferable Evidence.** Examples include hiring-related coordination without a formal recruiter title, workflow management in people-facing settings, structured stakeholder follow-up, screening-adjacent evaluation work, and multi-step internal-process coordination.
**7.4 Caution in Interpretation.** Transferable evidence shall be interpreted carefully and shall not be exaggerated beyond what the CV reasonably supports.

### Section 8. Role-Specific Evaluation Guidance
**8.1 Primary Screening Focus.** The central question is whether the candidate appears capable of structured talent coordination, evaluation support, and reliable execution in a fast-moving SME setting.
**8.2 Direct Title Non-Requirement.** A formal recruiter, talent specialist, people operations, or operations manager title is not strictly required where underlying experience credibly supports equivalent fit.
**8.3 Keyword Non-Equivalence.** Exact match with role wording shall not be treated as the sole basis of fit assessment.
**8.4 Borderline Candidate Logic.** Meaningful strengths with incomplete direct role-fit evidence should be treated as borderline rather than automatically negative.
**8.5 Interview-Relevant Uncertainty.** Uncertainty reasonably clarifiable through human interview should weigh in favour of continued review rather than premature exclusion.

### Section 9. Strategic Importance and Decision Sensitivity
**9.1 Strategic Importance of the Hire.** This role affects the organisation's ability to coordinate growth, maintain process reliability, and support talent-related execution.
**9.2 Consequence of Misclassification.** Incorrect screening may prematurely exclude a strong coordination candidate or advance one with weaker process ownership than required.
**9.3 Need for Careful Judgement.** Review shall involve active human inspection rather than passive reliance on a recommendation alone.

### Section 10. AI-Usable Referencing Clauses
**10.1 Citable Provisions.** The assistant may cite **Section 5.4** for evaluation or screening evidence, **Section 5.7** for fast-moving SME fit, **Section 6.2** for direct talent operations experience, **Section 7.2** for transferable evidence, **Section 8.3** for keyword non-equivalence, and **Section 8.5** for interview-relevant uncertainty.

### Section 11. Interpretation Rule
**11.1 Governing Interpretation.** This role description shall be interpreted in a way that prioritises underlying competence, structured judgement, and operational reliability over superficial wording matches.

**11.2 Screening Implication.** Where a candidate appears plausibly capable of performing the underlying responsibilities of the role, and where uncertainty remains interpretive rather than disqualifying, continued review shall be considered appropriate.
