# AI-Assisted Strategic Hiring Screening Policy

### Section 1. Short Title and Purpose
**1.1 Short Title.** This document shall be cited as the **Strategic Hiring Screening and AI-Assisted Recommendation Policy**.
**1.2 Purpose.** This Policy governs first-stage screening decisions for the role of **Strategic Talent Operations Partner**, and establishes standards under which an AI-assisted screening system may support, but shall not replace, human judgement.
**1.3 Policy Objective.** Screening shall be structured, fair, reviewable, and accountable, with attention to role fit, transferable evidence, procedural consistency, and human decision authority.

### Section 2. Scope and Applicability
**2.1 Scope.** This Policy applies to all first-stage screening decisions for the role identified in the corresponding role description, whether support is manual, digital, or AI-assisted.
**2.2 Applicability to AI-Assisted Screening.** An AI-assisted system may review candidate materials, compare them with role requirements, and produce a screening recommendation in accordance with this Policy, but shall act only in an advisory capacity.
**2.3 Human Oversight Requirement.** No AI-assisted recommendation shall be treated as a final decision. The human recruiter or hiring manager retains authority and responsibility for the final screening outcome.

### Section 3. Definitions
**3.1 Candidate.** A person whose CV, resume, or supporting application material is under review for the role.
**3.2 Required Qualification.** A capability, experience, or competence designated in the role description as necessary for satisfactory performance.
**3.3 Preferred Qualification.** A capability, experience, or competence designated as advantageous but not strictly necessary for initial progression.
**3.4 Transferable Evidence.** Evidence not using the precise wording of the role description but demonstrating substantially equivalent relevant competence, experience, or capability.
**3.5 Screening Recommendation.** One of the three permitted first-stage outcomes in Section 6.
**3.6 AI-Assisted Recommendation System.** A computational system that analyses candidate materials and produces a recommendation or explanatory output for recruiter use.
**3.7 Borderline Candidate Case.** A case with meaningful signs of role fit alongside unresolved uncertainty, ambiguity, or incomplete direct evidence.

### Section 4. General Screening Principles
**4.1 Structured Evaluation Principle.** Evaluations shall explicitly consider the role description, this Policy, and evidence in submitted candidate materials.
**4.2 Relevance Principle.** Only information relevant to role fit, qualifications, and capability to perform role responsibilities shall be considered.
**4.3 Non-Automation Principle.** AI-assisted recommendation shall not make screening an automated decision; human review is mandatory.
**4.4 Proportionality Principle.** Where evidence supports uncertainty rather than clear non-fit, the outcome shall reflect uncertainty rather than force premature exclusion.
**4.5 Accountability Principle.** The recruiter or hiring manager remains accountable for the final decision whether or not AI advice is followed.

### Section 5. Evaluation Standards
**5.1 Primary Evaluation Criteria.** Evaluate evidence of Required Qualifications; transferable or equivalent experience where direct wording is absent; structured judgement, screening, coordination, or evaluation-related work; stakeholder communication and cross-functional coordination; and independent execution, follow-through, and process reliability.
**5.2 Secondary Evaluation Criteria.** Preferred Qualifications, process improvement or workflow documentation, and relevant industry or organisational context may be considered but shall not override primary criteria without justification.
**5.3 Evidence-Based Assessment.** Assessment shall be based on the CV and accompanying materials. No qualification, experience, or competence may be assumed without reasonable record support.
**5.4 Uncertainty Treatment.** A capability not directly stated but reasonably suggested by adjacent experience shall be cautiously interpreted rather than automatically rejected.

### Section 6. Permitted Screening Outcomes
**6.1 Exclusive Outcome Set.** Permitted outcomes are **Reject**, **Advance to Human Interview**, and **Hold for Further Review**.
**6.2 Reject.** Reject only where evidence demonstrates clear mismatch across multiple Required Qualifications and transferable evidence is absent, weak, or insufficient for continued consideration.
**6.3 Advance to Human Interview.** Advance where most Required Qualifications are clearly met or strongly approximated by direct and transferable evidence and remaining uncertainty is minor and interview-resolvable.
**6.4 Hold for Further Review.** A candidate shall be assigned the outcome **Hold for Further Review** where the evidence indicates plausible role fit but material uncertainty remains, including cases in which meaningful strengths coexist with unresolved gaps, ambiguity, or indirect evidence.
**6.5 Borderline Cases.** If the candidate is neither clearly unsuitable nor clearly progression-ready, the default policy-preserving outcome is **Hold for Further Review**.

### Section 7. Transferable Evidence and Keyword Non-Equivalence
**7.1 Non-Identity of Wording and Competence.** Absence of exact role-description wording does not alone establish absence of underlying competence.
**7.2 Transferable Evidence Requirement.** Where CV terminology differs, assess whether the described experience provides credible evidence of the relevant capability.
**7.3 Prohibition on Exact-Match Rejection.** No candidate shall be rejected solely because their CV does not contain exact role-description keywords, where credible transferable evidence is present.
**7.4 Adjacent Experience Rule.** Coordination, structured documentation, workflow management, hiring support, stakeholder communication, or screening-adjacent tasks may be transferable evidence where they reasonably demonstrate the underlying competence.

### Section 8. Fairness, Restraint, and Evidential Limits
**8.1 Fairness Principle.** Avoid premature exclusion based on superficial wording differences, title absence, or non-standard expression of relevant capability.
**8.2 No Hallucinated Evidence.** An AI-assisted recommendation system shall not invent, embellish, or infer unsupported candidate qualifications.
**8.3 No Protected-Attribute Reasoning.** No recommendation or decision shall be based on actual or inferred protected personal characteristics.
**8.4 No Overstatement of Confidence.** Where the evidence is mixed or incomplete, the recommendation shall acknowledge uncertainty rather than presenting the case as conclusively resolved.

### Section 9. AI Recommendation Rules
**9.1 Advisory Role.** The system may produce a recommendation and rationale only in an advisory capacity.
**9.2 Document-Bound Reasoning.** A recommendation shall be grounded in the company context, the role description, this Policy, and the candidate CV.
**9.3 Recommendation Integrity.** Recommendation logic shall remain stable across presentation conditions; communication style, explanation detail, or interaction structure shall not alter substantive evaluation standards.
**9.4 Explainability Reference Rule.** A rationale should identify required criteria, transferable evidence, unresolved gaps, or policy principles and may cite governing sections directly.
**9.5 Permitted Policy References.** The AI may cite **Section 5.1**, **Section 6.4**, **Section 7.3**, **Section 8.4**, and **Section 10.1** in its rationale.

### Section 10. Human Decision Authority
**10.1 Final Decision Rule.** The final screening decision shall always remain with the human recruiter or hiring manager.
**10.2 Right to Override.** The human may accept, question, request clarification on, or override the AI-assisted recommendation.
**10.3 Verification Right.** The human may inspect the company context, the role description, this Policy, and the candidate CV at any time before deciding.
**10.4 Duty of Independent Review.** For a borderline or strategically important case, the recruiter shall exercise independent judgement rather than treat AI advice as self-sufficient.

### Section 11. Recommended AI Explanation Format
**11.1 Structure.** A recommendation should identify the outcome, strongest supporting evidence, strongest caution or uncertainty, and, where relevant, the governing Policy section.
**11.2 Illustrative Language.** The assistant may state that Section 7.3 prevents exact-keyword rejection, Section 6.4 applies to plausible candidates with material uncertainty, and Section 10.1 leaves the final decision with the recruiter.

### Section 12. Review, Escalation, and Human Determination
**12.1 Review in cases of uncertainty.** Where the available evidence does not clearly support either rejection or progression to human interview, the recruiter may assign the application to further review.
**12.2 Escalation principle.** Where the candidate presents relevant evidence of potential role fit but material uncertainty remains, the recruiter should prefer further inspection over premature exclusion.
**12.3 Human determination.** In all uncertain or contested cases, the final determination shall remain with the human recruiter or hiring manager.

### Section 13. Record and Audit Logic
**13.1 Recommendation Record.** Record the AI recommendation, rationale, policy sections referenced, and whether the human followed or overrode it.
**13.2 Auditability Principle.** The recommendation process should remain reviewable after the decision, including its grounds.

### Section 14. Closing Provision
**14.1 Governing Principle.** Interpret this Policy in favour of structured, fair, evidence-based human decision-making, with AI support not replacement.
**14.2 Summary Rule.** Where a candidate appears plausible but uncertain and transferable evidence exists, prefer cautious human review over premature exclusion.
